Chicago AirBnB
Price & Value Analysis

An interactive exploration of 7,300+ Chicago AirBnB listings built with Python and Altair. The project answers three questions a visitor would ask: which neighborhoods are affordable, how room type shapes the price you pay, and where the best-value listings hide (cheap and highly rated).

Data comes from Inside AirBnB, scraped September 2025. After cleaning — removing the ~5% of listings priced above $500/night that skew the market — 7,324 listings remain across 76 Chicago neighborhoods. All four charts below are fully interactive: hover for details, click legends to filter, and drag to brush-select regions.

Median Price by Neighborhood

Top 25 neighborhoods by listing count, sorted by median nightly price. Dashed line = city-wide median ($144). Hover a bar for exact figures.

Finding Near North Side, Loop, and Near South Side command a 40–80% premium over the city median, reflecting lakefront access and proximity to major attractions. Irving Park, Avondale, and Bridgeport sit well below median — strong options for visitors who don't need to be downtown every day.

Price Distribution by Room Type

Box = interquartile range · Whiskers = 1.5×IQR · Dots = outliers. Click a room type in the legend to highlight it.

Finding Entire home and hotel listings have similar medians (~$155–$165), but entire-home listings span a much wider range — budget apartments under $80 up to premium units above $400. Private rooms reliably come in around $80/night, making them the best option for solo travelers on a budget.

Price vs. Review Score — Finding the Sweet Spot

Each dot is one listing. Size = reviews per month (popularity). The green zone marks listings below the city median and above 4.7 stars. Click a room type in the legend to isolate it.

Finding The sweet-spot quadrant is densely populated — hundreds of listings are both cheaper than the median and rated above 4.7 stars. Large dots (high reviews/month) in the green zone indicate listings with a proven track record, not just a handful of lucky early reviews.

Availability vs. Price

Drag to brush-select any region of the scatter plot — the histogram below updates to show the price distribution of just those listings.

Finding There is no strong negative correlation between availability and price — hosts do not systematically discount for higher vacancy. Premium listings (>$300/night) cluster at low-to-moderate availability, consistent with high-demand properties that fill quickly without needing to drop their price.
